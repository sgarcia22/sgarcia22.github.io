VR Self-Defense

There are countless reports of people being sexually and physically assaulted everyday. This application hopes to reduce the number of those occurrences by offering a self-defense virtual reality class to the public for free. I worked on game flow, detecting sphere collision and checking if the player violated any rules or took too long to hit the spheres. I also helped set up U.I., initial scenes, music, Oculus Touch controls, and haptics. The project was developed in Unity3D in under 36 hours for the Swamphacks 2018 Hackathon and uses the Oculus Rift and Touch controllers.
